Grand Coulee Dam

Photo by Bureau of Reclamation

Located on the Columbia River in Washington State this dam was created by Mason-Walsh-Atkinson Kier Company and finished in 1942. Further add-ons of creating the third power plant was finished in 1974 to supply the Pacific Northwest with the growing demand of power. Standing at 550ft high and a length of 5233ft, this concrete gravity dam produces more than 21 billion kilowatts-hours of electricity each year(usbr). The Grand Coule Dam is the largest dam in the United States when it comes to generating power attracting many tourist each year. Clean energy such as renewable energy is vital for the future as it is cheaper and more environmentally friendly as the earth gradually gets warmer.

Even though this dam has been one of the greatest engineering projects in United States it does not come without a cost. There has been many proposal from environmental activist groups to find out ways to preserve endangered species such as Salmon and Trout as there numbers are dwindling. Currently, there has been many efforts by state and federal government to address the solutions of endangered species. No doubt this is an iconic dam that will possibly not taken down until its decommision in the future.

Chief Joseph Dam

Photo by US Army Corps of Engineers

Located on the Columbia River in Washington State 1.5 miles upriver from Bridgeport Washington it is the second largest hydroelectric power producer in the United States (US Army Corp of Engineers). this dam was authorized as Foster Creek Dam and Powerhouse for power generation and irrigation by the River and Harbor Act of 1946 and completed in 1955. The River and Harbor Act of 1948 renamed the project Chief Joseph Dam in honor of the Nez Perce chief who spent his last years in exile on the Colville Indian Reservation. Because of its lack of fish ladders. Since this is not able to store large amounts of water in it's resevoir, another dam called Wells Dam needs to flow water at the same rate.

The Chief Joseph dam attracts many visitors each year while many recreational acitvities excist around the lake and the area. It is a favotire spot for anglers which have major game fish such as Walleye, Rainbow Trout and triploid. Even though there is an abundant amount of game fish the Chief Joseph Dam blocks all Salmon from coming upstream which is one of the biggets reasons in there decline in population size. Balancing wildlife population and clean energy is important when it comes to the future.

Robert Moses Niagara Dam

Photo by Brian Chen

The Robert Moses Dam is located 4.5 miles from the iconic of the Niagara Falls. As it is New Yorks biggest energy producer providing up to 2.6 million kilowatts of clean energy from its massive turbine. The power plant is part of the New York Power Authority (NYPA). The dam began construction in 1957 and was finished in 1961. The dam was proposed by then president and governor Franklin D. Roosevelt in 1914. The hydroelectric has served many benefits such as producing cheap, safe and environmentally power. The Robert Moses Dam is one of cleanest producing energy generators in New York and has had many upgrades in in the past decades to keep hydroelectric power avaliable for all customers especially when it comes to the supplying electric vehicles as there popularity increases.

Economically the dam is valuable tool in New York's economy as it protects more than 27,200 jobs (the municipal) wiht more upgrades being put in to it. As dams provide a abundance of clean and renewable energy they come at an environmental cost. The geographic location of New York where it has waters coming from Canada provides a great source of constant energy but the impact of wild life will need to be assesed more to fully understand the wildlife impacts. Not enough research has been done but the dam is proving that is is much cheaper and more sustainable than using fossil fuels.

John Day Dam

Photo by Getty Images

The John Day Dam is located 28 miles east of The Dalles, Oregon just below the mouth of the John Day River. The dam began contruction in 1958 and was completed in 1971 which is the newest dam on the Lower Columbia watershed. Built and operated by the U.S. Army Corps of Engineers with the capacity to produce 2,160 mW from its 16 generators. The John Day Hydropower Dam is 1975 ft in length and 184 ft tall. The dam underwent major structural repairs in the lockgate 2010 and a fish passage which was documented by National Geographic. It is a cool documentary to see.

As the dam is located in the Lower Columbia River which is a major spawning ground for many endangered salmon and trout species there has been many projects to improve endangered species population. The U.S. Army of Engineers is obligated to to find ways to preserve, mitgate and protect endangered species under the Endangered Species Act. Efforts such as the creating a fish pathway has been succesful in redirecting and providing these aqautic species with thier spawning grounds to provide for other critical recreational activities that will boost the Oregons economy.

Hoover Dam

Photo by GrayLineLasVegas

The Hoover Dam is a concrete gravity dam in the located in Boulder City, Nevada in the Black Canyons of the Colorado River on the border between Nevada and Arizona. Constructed from 1931 to 1936, the Hoover Dam was testimony to the American people that great success can still be built even when the economy was at it one of its lowest points. This construction efforts took thousands of workers, many who travelled from many states to find work in the peak of the Great Depression. Hoover Dam holds the largest resevoir volume of any dam in the United States. Essentailly, it holds the whole Lake Mead. Th dam provides Power to California, Nevada & Arizona.

Hoover Dam fulfilled the goal of disseminating the one-wild Colorado River through the parched Southwest landscape, fueling the development of such major cities as Los Angeles, Las Vegas and Phoenix. To these day, the Hoover Dam is a well known place and familiar to everyone as it is always something great to talk about. Capable of irrigating 2 million acres, its 17 turbines generate 4 billion Kilowatts-hour of power, enough electricity to power 1.3 million homes.
